Research Overview

Importance of the Problem

Coastal geomorphology critically influences human settlements, ecosystems, and infrastructures. Changes in wave climate, sediment transport patterns, and shoreline dynamics have profound impacts on urban resilience, particularly in densely populated regions like Lake Michiganâ€™s shoreline near cities such as Chicago and Milwaukee.

Research Objectives

  • Characterize long-term directional wave climate from historical records (1979-2023).
  • Quantify the impact of coastal structures on sediment dynamics and shoreline evolution.
  • Develop predictive models for shoreline change to support coastal management and planning.

Methodology

Utilizing high-resolution aerial imagery, deep-learning algorithms, and advanced spatial analysis techniques (e.g., TIN, KNN, and Spline), my research systematically analyzes shoreline changes, wave conditions, and geomorphological responses over multiple decades.

Preliminary Findings

Initial analyses demonstrate distinct seasonal and spatial bi-directional wave climates, with northern wave directions dominating extreme wave events. Furthermore, urbanized shorelines with engineered structures show altered sediment transport patterns compared to natural shorelines, influencing erosion and deposition rates.
